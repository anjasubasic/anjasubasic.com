About Me

My name is Anja (pronounced AHN-yuh) and I am originally from Belgrade, Serbia. I currently live in Hanover, NH, where I am pursuing an M.S. in Computer Science. at Dartmouth College. Prior to coming to Dartmouth, I lived in Joplin, MO, where I got my undergraduate degrees in Computer Science and Music Performance/Percussion.

I enjoy running, travelling, good food and coffee, playing classical and jazz music and learning new stuff :)

Experience

I currently work as a research assistant for professor V.S. Subrahmanian at Dartmouth College. The project I am currently working on attempts to predict influence of membership and relationship changes in a terrorist network, and how knowledge about those patterns can be further used to destabilize a terrorist network.

Prior to this position, I worked at Leggett & Platt Inc. in Carthage, MO, as a C# software engineer. I started as an intern at the beginning of my senior year of college, in September 2016, and was hired as a full-time developer a month or so later. I accepted the position and went on to work as full-time engineer, while still finishing my senior year of college and working on coursework and my undergraduate thesis. At Leggett & Platt, I worked on ETL applications written in C#, large databases, and improving the new Enterprise Resource Planning system the company had just converted to - Microsoft Dynamics AX. Although big manufacturing corporations aren’t really my cup of tea, I was surprised to find myself enjoying development for such a large number of users and the challenges that come with it, as well as working with big data.

Before Leggett & Platt, I interned at a local Joplin software development company, PILR Tech, for about a year, where I gained a lot of my C# knowledge and some experience with web development. I spent most of my time there working on a Windows Store application and going through OOP training. I also worked independently on a simple website for a company initiative that has an objective to incorporate programming courses into the local high school and middle school curriculum, and get more kids who do not come from a technical background to try and enjoy coding.
